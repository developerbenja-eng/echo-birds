# Booker T. Washington State Park

## Why Booker T. Washington?

One of Tennessee's **historic African-American parks**, built during segregation to provide recreation for Black citizens in the Chattanooga area. The park's 353 acres on **Chickamauga Lake** offer lake access, forest trails, and important civil rights history.

## Birding

- **Osprey** — fishing over Chickamauga Lake
- **Northern Parula** — lakeside forest canopy
- **Winter waterfowl** on lake
- **Bald Eagle** — winter, scanning from tall trees
- **Great Blue Heron** — shoreline

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 40 sites
- **Pool**: Olympic-size (summer)
- **Trails**: 3 miles
- **eBird hotspot**: Booker T. Washington SP

**Best strategy**: Lake walk + forest trail. 1-2 hours. Pair with Harrison Bay.
