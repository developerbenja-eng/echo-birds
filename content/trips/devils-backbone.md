# Devil's Backbone State Natural Area

## Why Devil's Backbone?

**Tennessee's newest state park** — still opening in stages. A 1,500-acre preserve on a narrow **Highland Rim ridge** along the Natchez Trace Parkway in remote Lewis County. The "backbone" is a dramatic knife-edge ridge between two creek valleys.

This is a **developing park** with limited infrastructure. Current birding is along the ridgetop and existing trails as they're developed. The upland hardwood forest is mature and relatively undisturbed.

## Birding

The park's forest is primarily **upland oak-hickory on limestone ridges** with ravine hardwoods in the valleys:

- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest in ravines
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — ridgetop canopy
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy ridgetop edges
- **Scarlet Tanager** — upland canopy (breeding)
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — mature forest

**Note**: Trail development is ongoing. Check tnstateparks.com for current access. The Natchez Trace Parkway itself provides roadside birding opportunities through the area.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk
- **Entry**: Free
- **Trails**: Limited (developing)
- **Access**: Via Natchez Trace Parkway (no commercial traffic, scenic driving)
- **Cell service**: Poor (rural Lewis County)

**Best strategy**: Combine with a drive on the Natchez Trace Parkway, which passes through excellent forest habitat. Stop at overlooks + trailheads.
