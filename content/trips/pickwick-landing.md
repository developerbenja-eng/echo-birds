# Pickwick Landing State Park — Trip Guide

> 1,400 acres around **Pickwick Lake + Pickwick Dam tailwaters** in Hardin County, ~2 hours southeast of Memphis. Resort park with dam-tailwater birding.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Pickwick Landing is a **TVA resort park** centered on Pickwick Lake (Tennessee River reservoir, just north of the Mississippi/Alabama border). The **dam tailwaters below Pickwick Dam** are the main birding draw — cold oxygenated water attracts fish, which attracts **gulls, eagles, and fishing birds**.

---

## Target species

### Dam tailwaters (year-round, peak winter)
- **Bald Eagle** (reliable at dam)
- **Osprey** (summer)
- **American White Pelican** (winter flocks)
- **Ring-billed Gull**, **Herring Gull**, **Bonaparte's Gull** (winter)
- Rare: **Lesser Black-backed Gull**, **Little Gull** (exceptional)
- **Double-crested Cormorant** (year-round)
- **Common Loon** (winter)

### Lake
- **Waterfowl** (scaup, buffleheads, mergansers in winter)
- **Osprey platforms** (summer)

### Forest + park
- Standard upland breeders (Summer Tanager, Wood Thrush)
- Purple Martin colonies near lodging

---

## Where to bird

### Pickwick Dam tailwaters
**The spot.** Park just below the dam on the TN side or in the state park. **Eagles perch in snags, gulls wheel over the churning water, fish jump.**

### Marina + waterfront
Open-water scanning.

### Inn area
Purple Martins + open-country species.

---

## When to visit

| Season | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Dec–Feb (PEAK)** | Eagles + gulls + pelicans at the dam |
| **Mar** | Migration waterfowl + early raptors |
| **Apr–May** | Spring migrants + breeding Osprey arriving |
| **Jun–Jul** | Osprey nesting, summer birds |

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~2 hr southeast via US-72 → TN-57 → TN-22
- **County**: Hardin (near Counce, TN)
- **Amenities**: Pickwick Landing Inn, marina, golf, campgrounds
- **Admission**: Free
- **Pair with**: Shiloh National Military Park (nearby) for a Civil War + birding day
