# Pinson Mounds State Archaeological Park — Trip Guide

> 1,200 acres protecting **the largest Middle Woodland Native American mound complex in the U.S.** (17 mounds, 2,000+ years old) along the South Fork Forked Deer River in Madison County — 1.5 hours east of Memphis.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Pinson Mounds is **archaeology first**, but the park's **mosaic of mature forest + prairie restoration + river corridor** creates solid bird habitat. The **open mound-top grasslands** give you a different community than Memphis bottomland:

- **Grassland + edge birds** that aren't typical of closed-canopy bottomland parks
- **Mound-top views** for raptor watching
- **Mature hardwood forest** in the interior trail loop
- **South Fork Forked Deer River** — slow-flow bottomland stream

---

## Target species

### Open-mound + edge habitat
- **Eastern Meadowlark** (on the prairie restoration)
- **Eastern Bluebird** (nest boxes scattered)
- **Indigo Bunting** (fencelines)
- **Field Sparrow** (open edges)
- **Red-tailed Hawk** (perching on mounds)
- **Turkey Vulture** (soaring over mounds)
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** (forest edge)

### Mature forest trails
- **Pileated Woodpecker**, **Red-bellied Woodpecker**
- **Carolina Wren**, **White-breasted Nuthatch**
- **Summer Tanager**, **Red-eyed Vireo** (summer)
- **Yellow-billed Cuckoo** (rain crow)

### River bottomland
- **Prothonotary Warbler** (lower trails along river)
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** (spring migrant)
- **Belted Kingfisher**
- **Great Blue Heron**

---

## Why it's useful

Pinson offers **a rare habitat mix in West TN**: grassland-on-mounds + mature hardwood + bottomland. This creates **edge effects** that concentrate birds, especially:
- **Spring hawk migration** (soaring over open mounds)
- **Breeding grassland species** that are hard to find at Memphis parks
- **Family-friendly outing** — easy flat trails + world-class archaeology

---

## Where to bird

### Sauls Mound + surrounding prairie
The largest mound (72 ft tall) gives you a panoramic view. **Grassland species** on the approach + **raptors from above**.

### Ozier Mound
Circular mound area. **Bluebird trail + prairie plantings**.

### Eastern Citadel Trail loop
Interior forest walk through mature hardwood. **Standard forest species.**

### River Trail
Descends to the river corridor. **Bottomland species** + waterthrush potential.

---

## When to visit

| Season | What to see |
|---|---|
| **Apr–May** | Spring migration + breeding arrivals + eastern meadowlarks singing |
| **Jun–Jul** | Breeding season · indigo buntings, tanagers, cuckoos |
| **Sep–Oct** | Fall migration + raptor movement over mounds |
| **Nov–Mar** | Winter sparrows, meadowlarks present, grassland birds |

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~1.5 hr east via I-40 → US-45 south of Jackson
- **County**: Madison
- **Museum**: interpretive center with Native American cultural history
- **Pair with**: Middle Fork Bottoms (adjacent in the same county)
- **Trails**: ~6 miles total, mostly flat
