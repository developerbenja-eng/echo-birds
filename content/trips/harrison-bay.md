# Harrison Bay State Park

## Why Harrison Bay?

**Chickamauga Lake** (a TVA reservoir on the Tennessee River near Chattanooga) with a famous **Bald Eagle nest cam** that streams live during nesting season. The park is the best easily-accessible birding on Chickamauga Lake — winter waterfowl, nesting eagles, and forest birds.

## Birding

### Shoreline + Marina
- **Bald Eagle** — nesting pair (eagle cam), year-round
- **Osprey** — fishing over lake (spring/summer)
- **Winter waterfowl**: Common Loon, Ring-necked Duck, Bufflehead, grebes
- **Great Blue Heron** — year-round

### Forest trails (3+ mi)
- **Northern Parula** — canopy singer
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy edges
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest

## Seasonal strategy

**November–March** (★★★★): Eagle nest activity begins. Winter waterfowl on lake.

**April–May** (★★★): Eagle chicks. Spring breeding birds. Osprey arrives.

**Summer** (★★): Recreation lake. Dawn birding.

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 128 sites + group camp
- **Marina**: Full-service
- **Eagle cam**: Live stream at tnstateparks.com during nesting season
- **eBird hotspot**: Harrison Bay SP

**Best strategy**: Winter morning — scope lake + check eagle nest. 2-3 hours.

## Pairing

- **Booker T. Washington** (15 min south) — Chickamauga Lake
- **North Chickamauga Creek Gorge** (20 min north) — plateau gorge
