# Middle Fork Bottoms State Park — Trip Guide

> 2,300 acres of **bottomland hardwood forest** along the South Fork Forked Deer River in Madison County (east of Jackson, TN). ~1.5 hours east of Memphis. Underbirded and underexplored.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Middle Fork Bottoms is **newer + less-known** than Memphis-area bottomland parks, but it covers the same **West TN cypress-tupelo + bottomland-hardwood** habitat at a useful scale. The park's **underbirded status** makes it a candidate for **fresh eBird records** — your sighting might be a first county record.

The **Forked Deer River system** is classic West Tennessee slough country, similar to Wolf River but less developed.

---

## Target species

Same West TN bottomland suite as Wolf River + Big Hill Pond:

**Breeders (Apr–Aug):**
- **Prothonotary Warbler** (abundant in flooded woods)
- **Northern Parula** (in moss-draped canopy)
- **Yellow-throated Warbler** (along sycamore-lined river)
- **Acadian Flycatcher**
- **Kentucky Warbler** (creek hollows)
- **Summer Tanager**
- **Wood Duck** (ubiquitous)
- **Red-shouldered Hawk** (nest territories)
- **Belted Kingfisher**

**Winter (Nov–Feb):**
- **Hooded Merganser** (sloughs)
- **Wood Duck** (flocks)
- **Rusty Blackbird** (declining — possible in wet woods)

**Migration (Apr–May, Sep–Oct):**
- Full bottomland warbler wave
- Shorebirds at river mudflats (late summer/fall)

---

## How to bird it

This is a **low-development park** — fewer boardwalks, more dirt trails + paddle access. Best approached:
- **By kayak/canoe** on the South Fork Forked Deer River
- **By trail** in dry months (April–June)
- **By slow driving** the park roads + pulling over to listen

---

## When to visit

**May** for warbler migration + breeders at peak. **June–July** for resident swamp birds in full territory. **November** for duck migration. Avoid **peak mosquito season (July–Aug midday)**.

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~1.5 hr east via I-40 → US-45 south of Jackson
- **County**: Madison (east of Jackson)
- **Park amenities**: minimal — day-use, some trails
- **Pair with**: Pinson Mounds (nearby, also Madison County)
- **Bring**: kayak if you have one, insect repellent, binoculars
