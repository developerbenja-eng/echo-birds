# Pickett CCC Memorial State Park

## Why Pickett?

**19,200 acres of remote Cumberland Plateau forest** bordering Kentucky's Big South Fork National River — the most remote state park in Tennessee. Named for the **Civilian Conservation Corps** crews who built the park in the 1930s, Pickett preserves **old-growth pockets, natural bridges, and sandstone arches** in nearly pristine plateau forest.

The remoteness means **minimal disturbance** and excellent forest-interior bird populations.

## The landscape

- **Old-growth plateau forest** — mature oak-hickory with canopy gaps
- **Sandstone arches + natural bridges** — unique geological features
- **Arch Lake** — small CCC-era lake (12 acres)
- **Thompson Creek** — clear stream through forested gorge
- **Big South Fork gorge** — adjacent national river area

## Birding

### Natural Bridge Trail (2 mi — moderate)
Through old-growth forest to geological features.

- **Cerulean Warbler** — canopy of tall oaks along ridge
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — old-growth trees with cavities
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest sections

### Lake Trail + Thompson Creek (3 mi — easy to moderate)
Circles Arch Lake and follows the creek.

- **Hooded Warbler** — dense understory
- **Black-throated Green Warbler** — hemlock pockets
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — creek edges

### CCC Historic Area
Walk among the original 1930s CCC stone structures.

- **Eastern Phoebe** — nests on stone buildings
- **Chimney Swift** — overhead
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — surrounding forest

## Seasonal strategy

**May–July** (★★★★): Peak breeding. Remote + quiet — excellent dawn chorus. Best window.

**April** (★★★): Early breeders. Wildflowers.

**October** (★★★): Fall foliage. Late migrants.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 31 sites + CCC-era cabins (rustic, historic)
- **Trails**: 15+ miles
- **Cell service**: Very poor (most remote county in TN)
- **eBird hotspot**: Pickett CCC Memorial SP

**Best strategy**: Camp overnight for dawn chorus. Full morning, 4+ hours. Combine with Big South Fork.

## Pairing

- **Standing Stone** (30 min south) — large plateau-transition park
- **Big South Fork NRRA** (adjacent) — gorge wilderness (federal, not state park)
