# Harpeth River State Park

## Why Harpeth River?

**The Narrows of the Harpeth** — where the Harpeth River bends in a dramatic **horseshoe loop** around a narrow limestone ridge, nearly meeting itself. You can stand on the bluff and see the river on both sides, 200 feet below. This geological oddity creates a **concentrated riparian corridor** with excellent river birding.

The park is spread across **multiple non-contiguous access points** along 40 miles of the Harpeth River. The Narrows unit is the signature destination.

## The landscape

- **Harpeth River** — rocky, shallow river flowing through limestone bluffs
- **The Narrows** — dramatic river bend around a 200-ft bluff ridge
- **Historical tunnel** — 19th-century hand-cut tunnel through the ridge for a water-powered ironworks
- **Riparian forest** — sycamore, river birch, elm along banks
- **Bluff-top cedar/oak** — drier upland forest on limestone ridges

## Birding routes

### Narrows Trail (1 mi loop — moderate)
From the parking area, follow the bluff trail to the river overlook and down to the tunnel entrance.

- **Belted Kingfisher** — rattling along the river below the bluffs
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky stream edges, walking + bobbing
- **Yellow-throated Warbler** — sycamores along the river
- **Warbling Vireo** — canopy of riparian trees

### Montgomery Bell Tunnel Area
Walk along the river at the base of the Narrows.

- **Northern Parula** — canopy singer in streamside forest
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — WHEEP from upland forest
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest along the river

### Hidden Lake + McCrory Lane Access
Quieter sections of the park along the upper Harpeth.

- **Great Blue Heron** — wading in shallows
- **Green Heron** — skulking along wooded banks
- **Wood Duck** — quiet pools

## Seasonal strategy

**April–June** (★★★★): Breeding season. River species at peak. Waterthrush + vireos singing. Wildflowers on bluffs.

**September–October** (★★★): Fall migration along the river corridor. Warblers moving through.

**November–February** (★★): Winter residents. Kingfisher + woodpeckers. Quiet but pleasant river walks.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Parking**: Small lots at each access point
- **Trails**: Short trails at each unit (total ~5 miles across all access points)
- **eBird hotspot**: Harpeth River SP — Narrows

**Best strategy**: Start at the Narrows at dawn. Walk the bluff trail + river level. 2 hours. Pair with Nashville-area parks.

## Pairing

- **Radnor Lake** (30 min east) — forest + lake
- **Montgomery Bell** (20 min west) — upland forest
