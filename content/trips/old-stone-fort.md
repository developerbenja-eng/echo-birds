# Old Stone Fort State Archaeological Park

## Why Old Stone Fort?

**A 2,000-year-old Native American ceremonial enclosure** built where two forks of the Duck River converge, with waterfalls on both sides. The "stone fort" is actually a series of earthen walls enclosing a 50-acre peninsula — one of the most fascinating archaeological sites in the Southeast. The converging rivers + waterfalls create **excellent riparian birding** as a bonus.

The combination of **waterfall spray zones, river corridors, and upland forest** on 876 acres makes this a surprisingly productive birding stop.

## The landscape

- **Duck River forks** — Big Duck River and Little Duck River converge in the park
- **Two waterfalls** — Blue Hole Falls (15 ft) and Step Falls on the Little Duck
- **2,000-year-old earthworks** — Woodland-era ceremonial enclosure walls
- **Riparian forest** — sycamore, river birch, hemlock along river corridors
- **Upland hardwood** — oak-hickory on the plateau above

## Birding routes

### Enclosure Trail (1.3 mi loop — easy)
Follows the ancient earthwork walls with river views.

- **Belted Kingfisher** — rattling along both river forks
- **Warbling Vireo** — sycamores along the rivers
- **Northern Parula** — canopy above trail

### Waterfall Trail (1.5 mi — moderate, some stairs)
Descends to Blue Hole Falls and the river confluence.

- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky stream edges near waterfalls
- **Eastern Phoebe** — nests on rock ledges near falls
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — forest canopy above falls

### Museum Area + Open Grounds
The archaeological museum and surrounding lawns.

- **Eastern Bluebird** — open areas
- **Chimney Swift** — overhead
- **Red-tailed Hawk** — soaring above the plateau

## Seasonal strategy

**April–June** (★★★): Breeding season. River species active. Waterfalls at good flow.

**October–November** (★★★): Fall foliage over waterfalls. Late migrants.

**Winter** (★★): Quiet but atmospheric. River still attracts kingfishers.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 8am–sunset (park); museum 8am–4:30pm
- **Entry**: Free (museum free)
- **Camping**: Nearby (not in park)
- **Trails**: ~3 miles
- **Museum**: Excellent Woodland-era archaeology exhibits
- **eBird hotspot**: Old Stone Fort State Archaeological Park

**Best strategy**: 2-hour stop. Walk enclosure trail + waterfall trail. Visit museum. Combine with other parks.

## Pairing

- **Henry Horton** (30 min west) — more Duck River birding
- **Tims Ford** (20 min south) — reservoir lake birding
