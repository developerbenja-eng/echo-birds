# Sgt. Alvin C. York State Historic Park

## Why Sgt. York?

A **small historic park** (36 acres) in the remote Wolf River valley of Fentress County, honoring America's most decorated WWI soldier. Alvin York captured 132 German soldiers single-handedly in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive (1918). The park preserves his home, grist mill, and general store.

The surrounding **open farmland + edge habitat** in the Wolf River valley supports open-country species.

## Birding

- **Eastern Meadowlark** — farm fields around the park (declining — treasure each one)
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy edges
- **Eastern Bluebird** — fence posts and open areas
- **Red-tailed Hawk** — soaring over valley

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk (grounds); museum seasonal
- **Entry**: Free
- **Location**: Remote Fentress County — limited services

**Best strategy**: Quick historic stop. 30 minutes. Combine with Pickett CCC or Standing Stone.
