# Fort Loudoun State Historic Park

## Why Fort Loudoun?

A reconstruction of the **1756 British fort** — the first British fortification west of the Appalachians, built during the French and Indian War. The fort sits on **Tellico Lake** (a TVA reservoir on the Little Tennessee River). The history is the primary draw; the lake adds birding value.

## Birding

- **Osprey** — fishing over Tellico Lake
- **Bald Eagle** — winter resident
- **Winter waterfowl** on Tellico Lake
- **Great Blue Heron** — shoreline
- **Eastern Bluebird** — open fort grounds

## The fort

The reconstructed palisade fort with barracks, magazine, and blacksmith shop tells the story of British-Cherokee relations in the 1750s — alliance turned siege turned massacre. Fascinating history.

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Museum**: Excellent colonial-era exhibits
- **Trails**: Short
- **eBird hotspot**: Fort Loudoun SHP

**Best strategy**: Historic visit + lake scan. 1-2 hours. Pair with nearby Great Smoky Mountains.
