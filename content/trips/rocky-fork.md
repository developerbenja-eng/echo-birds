# Rocky Fork State Park

## Why Rocky Fork?

**2,000 acres of remote Southern Appalachian wilderness** in Unicoi County — Tennessee's newest major natural-area park. Dense rhododendron, steep ridges, and clear mountain streams. Limited development — this is backcountry birding.

## Birding

The park's elevation range (2,500–4,800 ft) supports elevation-stratified species:

- **Black-throated Blue Warbler** — rhododendron understory at upper elevations
- **Canada Warbler** — rhododendron thickets near streams
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest
- **Hooded Warbler** — dense mid-elevation understory
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — mountain streams
- **Ruffed Grouse** — reported but uncommon

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Trails**: Developing (check current conditions)
- **Facilities**: Minimal — primitive backcountry
- **Cell service**: Poor
- **eBird hotspot**: Rocky Fork SP

**Best strategy**: Full day backcountry hike. Experienced hikers only. Combine with Roan Mountain (45 min north).
