# Chickasaw State Park — Trip Guide

> 14,400 acres of **CCC-era upland forest + three lakes** in Chester County, ~1.5 hours southeast of Memphis. Classic West TN upland hardwood habitat at a manageable scale.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Chickasaw is a **Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC) park** — built in the 1930s as an upland pine/hardwood reclamation forest. The CCC planted lakes and pines into a former over-logged landscape. Today's habitat is a **mix of mature planted pine, regenerating oak-hickory, and three small lakes**.

This gives you:
- **Pine-oak mosaic** that's rare in West TN (Pine Warbler habitat!)
- **Small lakes** for waterfowl + Osprey
- **Extensive trail system** through upland forest
- **Less crowded than Memphis parks** most weekends

---

## Target species

### Summer breeders
- **Pine Warbler** (in pine stands — a species that's rare in Memphis)
- **Prairie Warbler** (shrubby cuts)
- **Yellow-breasted Chat** (brushy edges)
- **Indigo Bunting**, **Blue Grosbeak** (open edges)
- **Summer Tanager**, **Scarlet Tanager** (canopy)
- **Wild Turkey** (year-round, gobbling peaks April)
- **Whip-poor-will** (nocturnal, pine-oak ridges)
- **Chuck-will's-widow** (nocturnal, edges)

### Winter
- **Hooded Merganser**, **Wood Duck** (lake inlets)
- **Bald Eagle** (occasional lake visitor)
- **Sparrow flocks** along edges
- **Pine Siskin**, **Red-breasted Nuthatch** (irruptive — some winters only)

### Migration
- Full warbler passage
- Broad-winged Hawk kettles September

---

## Where to bird

### Lake Placid
Central lake with a loop trail. **Wood Ducks + Hooded Mergansers** in winter.

### Campground + picnic areas
Mature trees + edge habitat. Good for backyard feeder-style birding at CCC-era cabins.

### Pine stands
Listen for **Pine Warblers** singing their trill. Pine stands are scattered through the park.

### Powerline cuts + old-field edges
**Prairie Warbler**, **Yellow-breasted Chat**, **Blue Grosbeak** in summer.

---

## When to visit

| Season | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Apr–May** | Warbler migration, Pine Warblers singing, Whip-poor-wills nightly |
| **Jun–Jul** | Breeding species in full swing |
| **Sep–Oct** | Fall warbler passage, hawk watching |
| **Nov–Feb** | Lake waterfowl, winter sparrow flocks, irruptive finches some years |

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~1.5 hr southeast via US-64
- **County**: Chester (near Henderson, TN)
- **Amenities**: cabins, inn, campgrounds, swimming lake
- **Admission**: Free
- **Combine with**: Pinson Mounds or Big Hill Pond
