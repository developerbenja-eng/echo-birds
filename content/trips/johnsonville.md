# Johnsonville State Historic Park — Trip Guide

> 636 acres on **Kentucky Lake's southern arm** in Humphreys County, ~2.5 hours east of Memphis. Civil War site with **lake-edge waterfowl birding**.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Johnsonville is a **historic park first** — site of an 1864 Civil War battle where Confederate cavalry captured a Union supply depot via gunboats on the Tennessee River. The **reconstructed earthworks + riverside trails** overlook Kentucky Lake.

For birds, it offers:
- **Lake-edge waterfowl** in winter
- **Pair with Nathan Bedford Forrest** (directly across the lake by water)
- **Quiet historic-site walking** with forest birds

---

## Target species

### Winter
- **Bald Eagle** (lake fishing)
- **Osprey** (winter residents in mild years)
- **Hooded Merganser**, **Bufflehead**, **Ring-necked Duck**
- **Great Blue Heron**, **Great Egret**
- **Ring-billed Gull**

### Summer
- **Osprey** (nesting)
- **Summer Tanager**, **Eastern Kingbird**
- **Purple Martin** (colonial houses)
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** (small creek areas)

### Migration
- Warbler passage in wooded areas
- Waterfowl moving through

---

## Why combine with Nathan Bedford Forrest

Johnsonville sits **directly across Kentucky Lake from Nathan Bedford Forrest State Park**. They're **one continuous lake-edge complex** — so a single day trip can cover both for a richer waterfowl + eagle day.

---

## When to visit

**Winter** for waterfowl + eagles. **April–May** for spring migration.

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~2.5 hr east via I-40 → TN-13 north
- **County**: Humphreys
- **Amenities**: museum, interpretive trails, riverside access
- **Admission**: Free
