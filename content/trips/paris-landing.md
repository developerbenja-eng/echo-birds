# Paris Landing State Park — Trip Guide

> 841-acre peninsula on **Kentucky Lake** in Henry County, ~2.5 hours northeast of Memphis. Resort-style park with **open-water waterfowl viewing** + Bald Eagle winter flights.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Paris Landing is a **peninsula jutting into Kentucky Lake** — specifically the widest, deepest portion of the lake. This geometry makes it a **raft-waterfowl hotspot** in winter. The park is primarily a **lakeside resort** (inn, marina, golf course), but the **open-water scanning** opportunities are exceptional.

---

## Target species

### Winter (Dec–Feb) — peak
- **American White Pelican** (large flocks on open water)
- **Bald Eagle** (regular, often fishing offshore)
- **Common Loon** (deep water)
- **Horned Grebe**, **Pied-billed Grebe**, **Eared Grebe** (rare)
- **Canvasback**, **Redhead**, **Greater/Lesser Scaup**, **Ring-necked Duck** (diving duck rafts)
- **Bufflehead**, **Common Goldeneye**, **Hooded Merganser**, **Common Merganser**
- **Tundra Swan** (small numbers possible)
- **Red-breasted Merganser** (on open water)
- **White-winged Scoter**, **Surf Scoter**, **Black Scoter** (rare, but recorded)
- **Ring-billed Gull**, **Bonaparte's Gull** (flocks)
- Rare: **Little Gull**, **Black-legged Kittiwake** (exceptional)

### Summer
- **Osprey** (nesting on lake platforms)
- **Eastern Kingbird**, **Eastern Bluebird** (open park areas)
- **Purple Martin** (colonial houses near lodging)

---

## Where to bird

### The point of the peninsula
Scan open water with a spotting scope. **Duck rafts + pelicans + loons**.

### Marina breakwater
**Gull concentrations** in winter. Best spot for finding rare gulls among the Ring-billed flocks.

### Inn + resort lawns
Purple Martin colonies, bluebirds, open-park birds.

### Golf course edges
Open-country birds + raptor perches.

---

## When to visit

| Season | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Dec–Feb (PEAK)** | Waterfowl rafts, pelicans, eagles, rare gull potential |
| **Mar** | Early migrant Osprey, waterfowl moving north |
| **Apr–May** | Spring migration, breeding Osprey |
| **Jun–Jul** | Breeding Osprey, Purple Martin colonies |
| **Sep–Oct** | Fall waterfowl arrival, American White Pelican flocks |

---

## Why it's Kentucky Lake essential

If you're chasing **rare gulls, scoters, or uncommon grebes** in TN, Paris Landing is your highest-odds spot. The wide-open lake + deep water + concentrating geography produces the **richest pelagic-style winter birding** in the state.

**Bring a spotting scope.** Binoculars alone aren't enough for scanning the rafts offshore.

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~2.5 hr northeast via I-40 → US-641 → US-79
- **County**: Henry (between Paris, TN and the lake)
- **Amenities**: Paris Landing Inn, marina, golf course, campground
- **Admission**: Free
- **Pair with**: Nathan Bedford Forrest (south end of lake)
