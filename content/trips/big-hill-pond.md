# Big Hill Pond State Park — Trip Guide

> 4,138 acres of swamp forest, beaver wetlands, and bottomland hardwood in McNairy County, 1.5 hours southeast of Memphis. The **closest true cypress swamp to Memphis** — similar bird community to Reelfoot but at a much more accessible scale.

---

## Why it's different from Memphis

Memphis bottomland parks (Wolf River, Meeman-Shelby) give you cypress-tupelo habitat — but Big Hill Pond is **more focused, more flooded, and more wetland-dominant**. The park centers on:
- **Travis McNatt Lake** (165-acre human-made lake)
- **Dismal Creek swamp** (cypress + tupelo bottoms)
- **Beaver-created wetland complex** with cattail marshes
- **Upland forest ridges** around the water

The **beaver-activity wetlands** are the difference. Beaver dams create **shallow flooded areas with snags** — prime habitat for birds that need standing dead trees + water.

---

## Target species

### Wetland specialists (not reliable at Memphis bottomland parks)
- **Prothonotary Warbler** (abundant — arguably denser than Wolf River)
- **Yellow-crowned Night-Heron** (nests in the swamp)
- **Green Heron** (common around beaver ponds)
- **Wood Duck** (breeds + winters in flooded cypress cells)
- **American Bittern** (occasional in cattails)
- **Belted Kingfisher** (rattling calls along every waterway)
- **Hooded Merganser** (winter)

### Bottomland + upland forest
- **Barred Owl** (reliable in old cypress-tupelo stands)
- **Pileated Woodpecker** (large dead cypress snags = food)
- **Acadian Flycatcher** (breeding ravines + creek habitat)
- **Kentucky Warbler** (moist creek bottoms)
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** (smaller streams — stream specialist)
- **Summer Tanager**, **Scarlet Tanager** (canopy)

### Migration
- Spring warbler migration **peaks late April – mid-May**
- Fall migration Aug–Oct with shorebirds at lake drawdowns

## Where to bird

### Tower Trail / Observation Tower
A ~2.5-mile loop trail with a **70-ft observation tower** overlooking the swamp. **The best single view** of the cypress-tupelo canopy — great for scanning for Prothonotary Warblers and flycatchers in breeding season.

### Dismal Creek Swamp Trail
Boardwalk through the flooded cypress-tupelo. **Peak Prothonotary habitat.** Gets you inside the swamp canopy.

### Travis McNatt Lake trails
Around the lake edge — mix of open water, marsh edge, and wooded edge. **Kingfishers, herons, wood ducks**.

### Beaver pond areas
Scattered through the park wherever beavers have been active. These shallow flooded pockets attract **Green Heron, Prothonotary, occasional American Bittern**.

---

## When to visit

| Season | What to see |
|---|---|
| **Apr–May (PEAK)** | Warbler migration + breeders arriving · Prothonotary in full song · warbler wave |
| **Jun–Jul** | Breeding swamp birds at peak · Acadian Flycatcher, Kentucky Warbler singing |
| **Aug–Sep** | Fall migrants + post-breeding herons + shorebirds on lake mudflats |
| **Oct–Nov** | Winter waterfowl arriving · Hooded Merganser, Wood Duck flocks |
| **Dec–Feb** | Winter birds · Bald Eagle occasional · overwintering waterfowl |
| **Mar** | Early Louisiana Waterthrush, waterfowl pushing through |

---

## Why it's useful if you're Memphis-based

- **90-minute drive** from East Memphis (faster than Reelfoot)
- **Same bird community as Wolf River Greenway** but **less crowded + bigger**
- **Observation tower** gives you a view of the swamp canopy you can't get anywhere else
- **Breeding American Bittern** is rare elsewhere in southwest TN
- **Good warm-up trip** before a more ambitious Reelfoot or Frozen Head run

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~1.5 hr southeast via US-72 or TN-57
- **Nearest town**: Ramer, TN
- **Visitor center**: has map + birding list
- **Trails**: ~30+ miles total, mostly well-marked
- **Admission**: Free
- **Bring**: boots or water shoes, insect repellent (serious — it's a swamp), binoculars
