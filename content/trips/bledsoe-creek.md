# Bledsoe Creek State Park

## Why Bledsoe Creek?

A **small, quiet park on Old Hickory Lake** — best for **winter waterfowl** and **Bald Eagles**. At just 164 acres, it's not a destination park, but the sheltered inlet where Bledsoe Creek enters Old Hickory Lake concentrates **wintering ducks and grebes** in a scannable area.

North of Nashville in Sumner County, 30 minutes from Gallatin.

## The landscape

- **Old Hickory Lake inlet** — sheltered cove where Bledsoe Creek enters the reservoir
- **Lakeshore forest** — mature hardwoods along steep banks
- **Campground clearings** — open areas useful for sparrows + ground birds

## Birding

### Shoreline access
The main birding: scope the inlet from the boat ramp and shoreline access points.

- **Winter waterfowl**: Bufflehead, Common Goldeneye, Lesser Scaup, Hooded Merganser, Pied-billed Grebe
- **Bald Eagle** — winter residents, perch in lakeside trees
- **Great Blue Heron** — year-round

### Forest trails (short)
Short wooded trails through lakeshore forest.

- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — breeding season canopy
- **Northern Parula** — spring/summer canopy
- **Winter**: Hermit Thrush, Dark-eyed Junco, White-throated Sparrow

## Seasonal strategy

**November–February** (★★★): Winter waterfowl on inlet. Bald Eagle. Best window.

**April–May** (★★): Spring migrants in forest. Limited habitat.

**Summer** (★): Not a birding destination in summer.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 57 sites
- **Trails**: Short (< 3 mi total)
- **eBird hotspot**: Bledsoe Creek State Park

**Best strategy**: Winter morning, scope inlet for waterfowl. Combine with a Nashville-area birding day.
