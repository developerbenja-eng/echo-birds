# Cumberland Mountain State Park

## Why Cumberland Mountain?

A **CCC-era park** with some of the **finest Depression-era stone architecture** in the Tennessee state park system. The CCC built a beautiful stone dam, lodge, cabins, and bridges in the 1930s — all still in use. The park's 1,720 acres of plateau forest and 50-acre Byrd Lake provide solid breeding-season birding.

## Birding

### Byrd Lake Trail (2 mi — easy)
Circles the CCC-era lake through forest.

- **Northern Parula** — lakeside canopy
- **Hooded Warbler** — dense understory
- **Belted Kingfisher** — lake shoreline
- **Wood Duck** — quiet coves

### Pioneer Short Loop (3 mi — moderate)
Upland forest trail through plateau hardwoods.

- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — mature forest
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy edges

## Seasonal strategy

**May–June** (★★★★): Breeding season. Good forest birding.

**April** (★★★): Early breeders + migration.

**Winter** (★★): Quiet. CCC architecture is worth a visit year-round.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 145 sites + CCC-era cabins
- **Restaurant**: Park restaurant (in CCC-era lodge)
- **eBird hotspot**: Cumberland Mountain State Park

**Best strategy**: Morning lake + forest trail. 2-3 hours. Combine with Frozen Head (30 min north).
