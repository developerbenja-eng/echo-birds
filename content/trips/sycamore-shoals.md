# Sycamore Shoals State Historic Park

## Why Sycamore Shoals?

Where the **Overmountain Men** mustered before crossing the mountains to defeat the British at the Battle of Kings Mountain (1780) — a turning point of the American Revolution. The park preserves **Fort Watauga** (reconstruction) and the Watauga River shoals where treaties with the Cherokee were negotiated.

History is the draw. The Watauga River adds riparian birding.

## Birding

- **Warbling Vireo** — sycamores along the Watauga River
- **Belted Kingfisher** — river shoals
- **Eastern Phoebe** — fort structures
- **Great Blue Heron** — river shallows
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky river edges

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free (fort reconstruction + museum)
- **Trails**: Short riverside walks
- **Drama**: Outdoor drama "The Wataugans" (summer)
- **eBird hotspot**: Sycamore Shoals SHP

**Best strategy**: Historic visit + river walk. 1 hour. Pair with Roan Mountain (20 min east).
