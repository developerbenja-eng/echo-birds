# Big Cypress Tree State Park — Trip Guide

> 330 acres of bottomland hardwood protecting some of the oldest **bald cypress specimens** in Tennessee, in Weakley County 2.5 hours northeast of Memphis.

---

## Why visit as a birder

This is a **small but character-rich park** built around individual **ancient cypress trees** — some estimated at **1,300+ years old** (before the 1976 loss of the "Big Cypress" champion tree to lightning). The surviving grove is a remnant of the original virgin bottomland forest that once covered West Tennessee.

For birding, the park offers **typical bottomland West TN species** in a small, walkable setting:
- **Prothonotary Warbler** (classic swamp warbler)
- **Wood Duck** (on the cypress sloughs)
- **Pileated Woodpecker** (ancient snags = food)
- **Barred Owl** (resident)
- **Summer migrants**: Acadian Flycatcher, Great Crested Flycatcher, Red-eyed Vireo

---

## Why it's worth the drive

Honestly — mostly **for the trees**, not the birds. Birds are **similar to Big Hill Pond and Wolf River Greenway**, but at this park you get:
- **Old-growth cypress** at accessible scale (short trails)
- **Photo opportunities** of living 300–500 yr old trees
- A sense of what West TN's forest **used to look like** before logging

Pair this park with nearby **Reelfoot Lake** (~1 hr further north) for a full West TN birding day.

---

## Where to bird

- **Main boardwalk loop** (~0.5 mi) through the cypress grove
- **Bird-watching stands** positioned among the big trees
- **Slough-edge trails** for wood duck + kingfisher viewing

---

## When to visit

**April–June** for breeding warblers + Prothonotary in full song. **October** for fall color on the cypress (they turn rust-orange).

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~2.5 hr via I-40 east → US-45 north
- **County**: Weakley (Greenfield area)
- **Pair with**: Reelfoot Lake (1 hr north) for a 2-park day
- **Small park**: plan 1–2 hours of walking
