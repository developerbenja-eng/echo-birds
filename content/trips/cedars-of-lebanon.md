# Cedars of Lebanon State Park

## Why Cedars of Lebanon?

**Globally rare habitat** that exists almost nowhere else. The **Central Basin cedar glades** of Middle Tennessee are a **unique ecosystem** — open limestone pavements with thin soil, scattered Eastern Red Cedars, and endemic plants found nowhere else on Earth. This is one of the **few places in the world** with this specific geology + vegetation combination.

For birders, the cedar glades produce a **completely different species mix** than any forest park. The open scrub, rocky glades, and pine-cedar-hardwood mosaic support species that need **successional, shrubby habitat** — birds that are declining across the East as forests mature.

**9,420 acres** — Tennessee's largest state park east of Natchez Trace.

## The landscape

The park sits on the **Nashville Basin limestone** — Ordovician-age rock (~450 million years old) that weathers into thin, rocky soil. Where the soil is thinnest, trees can't grow, creating **cedar glades**: open, sunny, wildflower-rich openings surrounded by cedar forest.

**Key habitats**:
- **Cedar glades** — open limestone pavement with sparse vegetation, endemic wildflowers (Tennessee coneflower, glade cress). Prairie Warbler + Chat habitat at edges
- **Cedar-hardwood forest** — dense Eastern Red Cedar with understory of oak, hickory, elm
- **Pine plantations** — CCC-era planted pines (1930s). Pine Warbler territory
- **Mixed deciduous forest** — mature second-growth with rich canopy
- **Jackson Cave system** — underground drainage, cave entrance accessible by trail
- **Cedar Forest Trail glades** — the best accessible glade complex in the park

## Birding routes

### Cedar Forest Trail (4 mi loop — moderate)
**The must-do trail for glade birding.** Loops through cedar forest, crosses several open cedar glades, and passes through mixed hardwood.

**Target species**:
- **Prairie Warbler** — glade edges with shrubs + young cedars. Listen for ascending buzz: "zee-zee-zee-ZEE." Best May–July
- **Yellow-breasted Chat** — dense thickets at glade-forest transition. Listen for chaotic song medley
- **Pine Warbler** — CCC pine plantations along trail
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — hardwood canopy sections
- **Wild Turkey** — common throughout, often on glade edges at dawn

### Limestone Sinks Trail (2 mi — easy)
Flat trail through cedar forest with sinkholes. Less birdy but atmospheric.

- **Kentucky Warbler** — dense understory near sinkholes
- **Northern Parula** — canopy along wetter sections
- **Barred Owl** — resident in mature cedar-hardwood

### Dixon Merritt Nature Trail (1 mi — easy)
Short loop near campground. Good for evening/dusk birding.

- **Chuck-will's-widow** — begin calling at dusk (May–August). Stand at any forest edge at civil twilight and listen. "CHUCK-will's-WID-ow" repeated endlessly
- **Great Horned Owl** — resident, calls at dusk
- **Eastern Whip-poor-will** — overlaps with Chuck-will's-widow here; listen for higher-pitched, faster call

### Park roads at dawn
Drive slowly through the park at sunrise. Forest roads produce excellent birding.

- **Wild Turkey** — on roads at dawn
- **Yellow-throated Warbler** — tall trees along roads
- **Bald Eagle** — occasional flyovers

## Seasonal strategy

**May–June** (★★★★★): Peak breeding season. All target species singing. Cedar glade wildflowers blooming (Tennessee coneflower May–June). Prairie Warbler + Chat at peak vocal activity. Chuck-will's-widow calling at dusk. Best overall visit window.

**April** (★★★★): Early breeders on territory. Yellow-throated Warbler already singing. Migration adds diversity. Glade wildflowers beginning.

**July** (★★★★): Still singing but quieter after mid-month. Chuck-will's-widow still calling at dusk. Hot — go early.

**March** (★★★): Early arrivals. Yellow-throated Warbler first. Forest still open (no leaf-out), good visibility.

**August–September** (★★): Singing drops off. Some early migrants. Hot.

**October–February** (★★): Winter residents. Quiet. Hermit Thrush in forest. Cedar Waxwing flocks in cedar berries. Dark-eyed Junco on glades.

## What Cedars has that Memphis doesn't

Cedar glades don't exist in the Mississippi Delta. This is an **entirely different ecological world**:

| Feature | Cedars of Lebanon | Memphis area |
|---------|------------------|--------------|
| **Cedar glades** | Globally rare, endemic plants | None |
| **Prairie Warbler** | Breeds on glade edges | Absent |
| **Yellow-breasted Chat** | Common at glade margins | Rare |
| **Chuck-will's-widow** | Common at dusk | Very rare |
| **Pine Warbler** | CCC pine plantations | Sparse |
| **Geology** | Exposed limestone pavement | Alluvial clay |
| **Endemic plants** | TN coneflower, glade cress | None |

## The cedar glade story

Cedar glades are **Tennessee's most unique contribution to global biodiversity**. These open limestone barrens support **19 endemic plant species** found nowhere else — including the **Tennessee coneflower** (*Echinacea tennesseensis*), which was one of the first plants listed under the Endangered Species Act.

The glades persist because the **limestone bedrock is too close to the surface** for deep-rooted trees. Only shallow-rooted Eastern Red Cedars and drought-adapted wildflowers survive. The result is a patchwork of open rocky clearings that function like tiny prairies surrounded by forest — perfect for shrubland birds like Prairie Warbler and Chat.

Development has destroyed **90%+ of original cedar glade habitat** in the Nashville Basin. The park protects the largest remaining complex.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset, year-round
- **Entry**: Free, no fee
- **Camping**: 117 sites + cabins (reserve at tnstateparks.com)
- **Dogs**: Allowed on leash on most trails
- **Trails**: 12 miles total
- **Facilities**: Visitor center, pool (summer), nature programs
- **eBird hotspot**: Cedars of Lebanon State Park
- **Cell service**: Variable (Wilson County — rural)

**Best strategy**: Arrive at dawn. Walk Cedar Forest Trail for glade-edge species (Prairie Warbler, Chat). Drive roads for Turkey + canopy species. Return at dusk to Dixon Merritt Trail for Chuck-will's-widow. Full day.
