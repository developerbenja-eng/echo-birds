# Natchez Trace State Park — Trip Guide

> **48,000 acres** — the **largest state park in Tennessee**. Four lakes, 250+ miles of equestrian/hiking trails, upland oak-hickory forest, and ~2 hours east of Memphis. Dramatic scale + habitat diversity make it underused by Memphis birders.

---

## Why it's different from Memphis

Memphis's parks are **small and bottomland-focused**. Natchez Trace is **huge and upland-focused** — you're in CCC-built **West Tennessee upland hardwood country** with mature oak-hickory ridges, small creeks, and **four constructed lakes** (Cub Creek, Maple Creek, Brown's Creek, Pin Oak).

Compared to Memphis parks:
- **15× larger than Meeman-Shelby** (48,000 vs 12,500 acres)
- **Less bottomland, more upland** — different bird community
- **4 lakes add waterfowl + osprey habitat** that Memphis bottoms don't have
- **Prairie-restoration areas** along some ridges

---

## Target species

### Summer breeders (Apr–Aug)
- **Osprey** (summer residents on the lakes — nest platforms)
- **Broad-winged Hawk** (forested ridges)
- **Red-shouldered Hawk** (along streams)
- **Wood Thrush** (mature interior forest)
- **Yellow-throated Vireo** (canopy)
- **Prairie Warbler** (shrubby clearings + power-line cuts)
- **Blue-winged Warbler** (young forest + old field edges)
- **Yellow-breasted Chat** (brushy cuts)
- **Indigo Bunting** (roadside edges)
- **Wild Turkey** (upland forest)
- **Whip-poor-will** (nocturnal breeder — upland ridges)

### Winter (Nov–Feb)
- **Bald Eagle** (on/near the lakes)
- **Common Loon** (deep lakes, uncommon)
- **Hooded Merganser**, **Bufflehead**, **Ring-necked Duck** (lake ducks)
- **Dark-eyed Junco** + winter sparrow flocks

### Migration
- Full warbler passage April–May + September
- **Broad-winged Hawk kettles** September

---

## Where to bird

### Cub Creek Lake area
Best for lake-edge waterfowl + Osprey nests + fall hawk movement. **Campground + picnic loops** have mature trees + lake-edge scanning.

### Brown's Creek Lake (Fairview area)
Smaller lake, good for Wood Ducks and Hooded Mergansers in winter.

### Pin Oak Lake + campground
Largest lake. **Osprey platforms** here in summer. **Common Loon** possible in winter.

### Forest trails (Redbud Trail, Red Leaves Trail)
Interior hardwood forest. Wood Thrush, Yellow-throated Vireo, Wild Turkey.

### Power-line cuts + open areas
**Prairie Warbler**, **Yellow-breasted Chat**, **Blue-winged Warbler** in spring + summer. **Shrubland specialists** you won't find in Memphis parks.

---

## When to visit

| Season | Best for |
|---|---|
| **Apr–May** | Spring warbler migration + breeding arrivals |
| **May–Jun** | Breeding at peak · Prairie Warblers + Chats on power lines |
| **Jun–Jul** | Osprey chicks on lake platforms · Whip-poor-wills at dusk |
| **Sep–Oct** | Fall warbler passage + Broad-winged kettles |
| **Nov–Feb** | Lake waterfowl + Bald Eagle · winter sparrows in fields |
| **Mar** | Early-migrant Osprey + first Louisiana Waterthrushes |

---

## Trail picks

### Redbud Trail (1.5 mi)
Loop through mixed hardwood near the main campground. Good introduction.

### Pin Oak Trail (3 mi)
Circles Pin Oak Lake. Osprey + waterfowl.

### Fern Point Trail (1 mi)
Short nature walk with interpretive signs.

### Long-distance trails
**Cub Creek Trail** (21 mi) + **Jack Creek Trail** (40+ mi backcountry) for multi-day birding.

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~2 hours east via I-40 exit 116 (Wildersville)
- **County**: Henderson (primary), extending into Benton/Carroll
- **Nearest town**: Wildersville, TN
- **Park HQ**: Cub Creek area · visitor center + map
- **Lodging**: Natchez Trace Inn + cabins + campgrounds
- **Admission**: Free
- **Pair with**: Nathan Bedford Forrest or Paris Landing (same Kentucky Lake day)

---

## Why it's worth the drive from Memphis

This is Tennessee's **biggest single contiguous upland forest** — a sharp contrast with Memphis's fragmented bottomland. If you want:
- **Prairie Warbler** (without mountains)
- **Wild Turkey** close to Memphis
- **Osprey on nesting platforms**
- A long-form forest-bird day without a long drive

Natchez Trace is the answer.
