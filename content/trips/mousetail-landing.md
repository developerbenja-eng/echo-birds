# Mousetail Landing State Park — Trip Guide

> 1,247 acres of **Tennessee River bluffs** in Perry County, ~3 hours east of Memphis. Remote, quiet, underbirded.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Mousetail Landing is **one of Tennessee's most remote state parks** — the middle-river bluff country, far from any major town. The name comes from tannery workers who processed cowtails there — they said the hair looked like "mouse tails."

For birds, you get:
- **Tennessee River bluff views** — high vantage for scanning
- **Mature bluff-top hardwood** forest
- **Bottomland below the bluffs**
- **Small creeks** draining into the river

---

## Target species

Standard Tennessee River bluff birding:
- **Wild Turkey** (forest + edges)
- **Summer breeders**: Wood Thrush, Scarlet Tanager, Summer Tanager, Acadian Flycatcher
- **Bluff-top raptors**: Red-tailed Hawk, Turkey Vulture, Black Vulture
- **Winter waterfowl** on the river
- **Migration warblers** along bluff edges

---

## Why the drive

Honestly — only if you're doing a **Tennessee River tour** or want solitude. Most Memphis-based birders will get similar species at closer parks (Natchez Trace, Nathan Bedford Forrest). Mousetail's value is **quiet + wilderness feel + first-county records** if you're a serious eBirder.

---

## When to visit

**April–June** for spring migration + breeders. **November–February** for river waterfowl.

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~3 hr east via I-40 → TN-13 south
- **County**: Perry (near Linden, TN)
- **Amenities**: campgrounds, cabins, ranger-led programs
- **Admission**: Free
- **Trails**: ~12 miles
