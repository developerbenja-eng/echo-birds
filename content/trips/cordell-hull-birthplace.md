# Cordell Hull Birthplace State Park

## Why Cordell Hull?

A **small historic park** (59 acres) honoring the Tennessee native who served as **US Secretary of State for 11 years** and won the **Nobel Peace Prize** for his role in founding the United Nations. The park preserves his log cabin birthplace surrounded by upland forest.

Not a birding destination — but worth a short stop when visiting Standing Stone or the Upper Cumberland.

## Birding

- **Wood Thrush** — forest around cabin site
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy edges
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — mature forest

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk (grounds); museum 9am–5pm (seasonal)
- **Entry**: Free
- **eBird hotspot**: Cordell Hull Birthplace SP

**Best strategy**: 30-minute stop en route to Standing Stone.
