# Port Royal State Park

## Why Port Royal?

A **tiny historic park** (34 acres) at the confluence of the **Red River and Sulphur Fork Creek** near the Kentucky border. Port Royal was a thriving 19th-century river town — now a quiet riverside preserve with covered bridge remains and riparian habitat.

This is a **quick stop**, not a destination. The river confluence creates a pocket of riparian birding.

## Birding

The park's 34 acres are mostly **Red River bottomland** with mature sycamores and riparian forest:

- **Belted Kingfisher** — resident along the river
- **Warbling Vireo** — sycamore canopy along Red River
- **Great Blue Heron** — wading at the confluence
- **Eastern Phoebe** — under the covered bridge structure
- **Wood Duck** — quiet river pools

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk
- **Entry**: Free
- **Trails**: Short (< 1 mile)
- **Historic features**: Covered bridge site, masonic hall ruins

**Best strategy**: 30-minute stop. Walk to river confluence, scan for riparian species. Combine with Dunbar Cave (20 min southwest).

## Pairing

- **Dunbar Cave** (20 min southwest) — cave + forest
