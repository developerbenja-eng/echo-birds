# Long Hunter State Park

## Why Long Hunter?

**Percy Priest Lake + cedar glades** in one park. Long Hunter gives you two distinct birding experiences: **lakeside winter waterfowl** scanning from Couchville Cedar Glade's exposed shoreline, and **breeding-season forest birds** in the park's interior deciduous woodland and glade edges.

2,600 acres on the southern shore of J. Percy Priest Lake, 15 minutes from downtown Nashville.

## The landscape

- **Percy Priest Lake shoreline** — rocky shores + coves, winter waterfowl staging
- **Couchville Cedar Glade** — a State Natural Area within the park, globally rare cedar glade with endemic plants
- **Mature deciduous forest** — cove hardwoods with rich canopy
- **Lake peninsulas** — Sellars Farm day use area extends into the lake

## Birding routes

### Couchville Cedar Glade Trail (1 mi loop — easy)
Walk the glade boardwalk + loop trail through the best accessible cedar glade in the park. Lake views from the bluff edge.

- **Yellow-breasted Chat** — glade-forest edges (breeding season)
- **Prairie Warbler** — if shrubby edges are present
- **Wild Turkey** — glade edges at dawn
- **Winter**: scan lake from bluff overlook for diving ducks, Bald Eagle

### Day Loop Trail (5.5 mi — moderate)
Forest trail through interior hardwoods with lake glimpses.

- **Northern Parula** — canopy along creek crossings
- **Yellow-throated Warbler** — sycamores near water
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — WHEEP calls from canopy
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest sections

### Sellars Farm Day Use Area
Paved access to lake shore. Best for winter waterfowl scoping.

- **Bald Eagle** — perched along lake edge
- **Winter waterfowl**: Bufflehead, Ring-necked Duck, Ruddy Duck, Hooded Merganser
- **Great Blue Heron** — year-round along shore

## Seasonal strategy

**November–February** (★★★★): Winter waterfowl peak. Scan lake from Sellars Farm + Couchville bluff. Bald Eagle active.

**April–May** (★★★★): Migration + breeding. Warbler diversity in forest. Chat + Great Crested Flycatcher on territory.

**June–August** (★★): Hot, quiet. Early morning only.

**September–October** (★★★): Fall migration through forest.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Trails**: 12+ miles
- **Facilities**: Restrooms at day-use areas, boat ramps
- **eBird hotspot**: Long Hunter State Park

**Best strategy**: Winter morning — scope lake from Sellars Farm, walk Couchville Cedar Glade loop. Spring — Day Loop Trail at dawn.

## Pairing

- **Radnor Lake** (25 min west) — best paired for a Nashville birding day
- **Cedars of Lebanon** (30 min east) — cedar glade comparison
