# Meeman-Shelby Forest State Park

## Why Meeman-Shelby?

**Memphis's premier birding park** and a member of the **Big Five**. 12,539 acres of **Mississippi River bluff forest and bottomland hardwood** — the largest state park in the Memphis metro area. This is the most accessible wild forest in the region, dropping from loess bluffs down to the Mississippi floodplain.

**Home turf** — no driving required. This is where Memphis birders go most weekends.

## The landscape

- **Chickasaw Bluffs** — 100-ft loess bluffs above the floodplain, mature hardwood forest
- **Bottomland hardwood** — tupelo, cypress, sweetgum in the floodplain
- **Poplar Tree Lake** — oxbow lake in the bottomland
- **Mississippi River** — visible from bluff overlooks (when water is high)

## Birding routes

### Woodland Trail (1.5 mi — easy)
Through mature bluff forest.

- **Prothonotary Warbler** — golden flash in bottomland near water
- **Barred Owl** — resident, often vocal in daytime. "Who cooks for you?"
- **Red-shouldered Hawk** — screaming from bluff forest
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — loud laughing calls, rectangular excavations

### Poplar Tree Lake
Walk the lake edge and scan.

- **Wood Duck** — abundant in quiet waters
- **Great Blue Heron** — wading
- **Belted Kingfisher** — rattling over lake
- **Green Heron** — skulking along wooded banks

### Pioneer Trail + Bluff Trail (5+ mi — moderate)
Longer trails through bluff and bottomland.

- **Mississippi Kite** — soaring over canopy (summer)
- **Ruby-throated Hummingbird** — garden area near nature center
- **Summer Tanager** — canopy in breeding season
- **Northern Parula** — canopy above trails

## Seasonal strategy

**April–June** (★★★★★): Peak breeding. Prothonotary Warbler singing. All residents active. Kites soaring.

**September–October** (★★★★): Fall migration. Warblers moving through canopy.

**March** (★★★★): Early migrants. Waterthrush on streams. Woodpeckers drumming.

**Winter** (★★★): Residents — owls, woodpeckers. Winter sparrows. Quiet forest walks.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 49 sites + group camp
- **Trails**: 20+ miles
- **Nature center**: Exhibits, programs
- **eBird hotspot**: Meeman-Shelby Forest State Park

**Best strategy**: Dawn walk on Woodland Trail + Poplar Tree Lake. 3 hours. Go weekly.
