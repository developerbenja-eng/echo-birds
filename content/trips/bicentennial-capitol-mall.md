# Bicentennial Capitol Mall State Park

## Why Bicentennial Capitol Mall?

An **urban migrant trap** in downtown Nashville. At only 19 acres, this is Tennessee's smallest state park — but its **open lawns, fountains, and scattered trees** surrounded by city blocks create a **migrant magnet** during spring and fall. Tired birds dropping out of migration over Nashville land in the only green space they can find.

Not a destination birding park, but a **fun urban-birding stop** if you're in Nashville.

## The landscape

- **Mowed lawns + scattered trees** — the entire park
- **Fountains + water features** — attract migrants
- **Capitol Hill** overlook to the south
- Surrounded by **downtown Nashville buildings** — creates the migrant-trap effect

## Birding

### Migration season (April–May, September–October)
The park's birding value is entirely about **migrant fallouts**:
- **Warblers** in scattered trees — anything moving through Middle TN can drop in
- **Chimney Swift** — swirling overhead in large flocks, especially fall staging
- **Mississippi Kite** — urban Nashville has a growing breeding population; watch overhead
- **Thrushes** — Swainson's, Gray-cheeked on lawns during migration
- **Sparrows** — White-throated, Song Sparrow in brushy edges

### Year-round urban species
- **Peregrine Falcon** — nests on downtown Nashville buildings (AT&T tower)
- **Cooper's Hawk** — urban Nashville has high density
- **Common Nighthawk** — summer evenings overhead

## Seasonal strategy

**April–May** (★★★): Spring migration. Check after weather fronts for fallouts.

**September–October** (★★★): Fall migration staging. Chimney Swift flocks overhead.

**Other months** (★): Urban species only. Not worth a special trip.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk, year-round
- **Entry**: Free
- **Parking**: Metered street parking + nearby garages
- **Facilities**: Restrooms, interpretive exhibits about TN history + geography
- **Note**: This is a history/civics park with birding as a bonus — the 31 fountains (one per TN river), 200-ft granite map, and WWII memorial are the primary attractions

**Best strategy**: Quick 30-min walkthrough during migration season, combined with other Nashville birding stops. Not worth a standalone trip from Memphis.

## Pairing

- **Radnor Lake** (20 min south) — the real birding. Always pair with Radnor.
- **Warner Parks** (15 min west) — 3,100 acres of forest for serious birding
