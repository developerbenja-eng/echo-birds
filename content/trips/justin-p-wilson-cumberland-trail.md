# Cumberland Trail State Scenic Trail

## Why the Cumberland Trail?

A **300-mile long-distance trail** running the length of the Cumberland Plateau from Cumberland Gap to Signal Mountain near Chattanooga. When complete, it will be **Tennessee's premier long-distance trail**. Currently ~200 miles are open in segments.

**Not a traditional state park** — this is a linear trail corridor traversing some of Tennessee's wildest plateau terrain. Each segment passes through gorges, cliff edges, and mature forest that supports plateau breeding species.

## Birding

The trail traverses classic Cumberland Plateau gorge-and-rim habitat:

- **Cerulean Warbler** — gorge-rim sections (multiple locations)
- **Worm-eating Warbler** — steep gorge slopes
- **Hooded Warbler** — forest understory
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — stream crossings
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — mature forest throughout

**Best birding segments**:
- **Frozen Head connector** — overlaps with Frozen Head trails
- **Obed River section** — remote gorge
- **Signal Mountain** — southern terminus near Chattanooga

## Logistics

- **Access**: Multiple trailheads across 10+ counties
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: Backcountry campsites along trail
- **Information**: cumberlandtrail.org for current conditions + open segments
- **Warning**: Backcountry trail. Some segments remote. Check conditions.

**Best strategy**: Pick a segment near another park you're visiting. Not a standalone birding destination — best as a supplement.
