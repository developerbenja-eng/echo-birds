# Seven Islands State Birding Park

## Why Seven Islands?

**Tennessee's only state park officially designated for birding** — and it delivers. Built on former farmland along the **French Broad River** near Knoxville, Seven Islands combines **grassland, river, forest edge, and wetland** habitats in 416 acres. With **190+ species recorded**, it's one of the most productive birding sites in East Tennessee.

What makes it unique among Tennessee state parks: **grassland habitat**. Most TN parks are forested. Seven Islands preserves **open fields + grassland** — supporting species that are rare elsewhere in the state park system: grassland sparrows, meadowlarks, raptors, and the occasional rare visitor.

## The landscape

- **Open grassland + old farm fields** — the signature habitat. Mowed and managed for grassland birds
- **French Broad River** frontage — 1.5+ miles of river shore
- **Forest edge** — mixed hardwood along margins
- **Wetland areas** — seasonally wet areas attracting shorebirds
- **Seven river islands** — the park's namesake, visible from shore

## Birding routes

### Grassland Loop (2 mi — easy, flat)
Walk through the open fields. **The core birding experience.**

- **Eastern Meadowlark** — singing from field fences (declining species — this is one of TN's best spots)
- **Eastern Bluebird** — nest boxes throughout fields
- **Red-tailed Hawk** — soaring over fields year-round
- **American Kestrel** — hovering over fields (winter/migration)
- **Northern Harrier** — low coursing over grassland (winter)
- **Short-eared Owl** — dusk in winter (rare but reported)
- **Grasshopper Sparrow** — breeding in fields (uncommon)
- **Savannah Sparrow** — winter grassland flocks

### River Trail (1.5 mi — easy)
Follows the French Broad River shore.

- **Bald Eagle** — resident pair, perched in tall riverbank trees
- **Osprey** — fishing over the river (spring/summer)
- **Belted Kingfisher** — river resident
- **Great Blue Heron** — wading in shallows
- **Sandhill Crane** — winter staging (November–February), visible in fields near river

### Forest Edge Trail (1 mi — easy)
Along the woods bordering the fields.

- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy edge, "drink-your-tea"
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy edge
- **Indigo Bunting** — singing from treetop perches at field edge
- **Yellow-breasted Chat** — dense thickets (breeding)

### Observation Areas
Multiple birding platforms with benches and scopes.

- **Hawk-watching** — fall migration (September–November)
- **Sparrow flocks** — winter grassland viewing
- **Crane staging** — winter river-field viewing

## Seasonal strategy

**April–June** (★★★★★): Peak breeding. Meadowlark singing. Grassland sparrows on territory. Osprey + Bald Eagle active. Wildflowers.

**September–November** (★★★★★): **Fall migration peak.** Hawk migration over fields. Sparrow flocks building. Sandhill Cranes arriving. One of the best fall birding windows in East TN.

**December–February** (★★★★): Winter raptors (Harrier, Kestrel). Sandhill Cranes in fields. Sparrow flocks. Short-eared Owl at dusk (some years).

**July–August** (★★): Hot + quiet. Dawn only.

## What Seven Islands has that most TN parks don't

**Grassland** — a habitat that has been almost entirely eliminated in Tennessee:

| Feature | Seven Islands | Most TN state parks |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Open grassland** | 200+ acres managed | None or minimal |
| **Eastern Meadowlark** | Reliable breeding | Rare/declining |
| **Grassland sparrows** | Multiple species | Absent |
| **Northern Harrier** | Winter regular | Very rare |
| **Raptors over fields** | Excellent | Limited |
| **Designated birding park** | Yes — the only one | No |

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk, year-round
- **Entry**: Free
- **Parking**: Ample
- **Trails**: 5+ miles, all flat and accessible
- **Facilities**: Restrooms, observation platforms, benches
- **Binocular loaners**: Available at trailhead kiosk (check availability)
- **Cell service**: Good (Knox County)
- **eBird hotspot**: Seven Islands State Birding Park (one of the most-reported in East TN)
- **Birding programs**: Regular guided bird walks led by park naturalists

**Best strategy**: Dawn walk through grassland loop → river trail. 3 hours. Bring a scope for river scanning. In winter, stay through dusk for possible Short-eared Owl.
