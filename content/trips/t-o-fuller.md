# T.O. Fuller State Park

## Why T.O. Fuller?

One of the first state parks in the nation **built for African Americans** (1938, during segregation). Named for **Dr. Thomas O. Fuller**, a prominent Black educator and Tennessee state senator. The park's 1,138 acres of **bottomland forest** in south Memphis provide accessible urban-edge birding with important civil rights history.

Adjacent to the **C.H. Nash Museum at Chucalissa** — a Mississippian-era Native American archaeological site (1000–1500 AD).

## Birding

### Forest trails (3+ mi)
- **Barred Owl** — resident in mature forest
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — loud calls through forest
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy (breeding season)
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy understory
- **Northern Parula** — canopy singer

### Open areas + edges
- **Eastern Bluebird** — open areas
- **Indigo Bunting** — forest edges (summer)
- **Red-tailed Hawk** — soaring
