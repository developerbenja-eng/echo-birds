# Cummins Falls State Park

## Why Cummins Falls?

A **dramatic 75-foot waterfall** on the Blackburn Fork — Tennessee's 8th largest waterfall by volume. The gorge hike to the base of the falls passes through excellent **riparian forest** with classic gorge-breeding species.

Only 282 acres, but the gorge corridor concentrates birds.

## Birding

### Gorge Trail (1.5 mi one-way — strenuous, river crossing)
Descends to the base of the waterfall through forested gorge.

- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky stream edges in gorge, walking + bobbing
- **Northern Parula** — canopy above gorge
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest, dawn chorus
- **Eastern Phoebe** — rock ledges near falls

### Rim Trail (0.5 mi — easy)
Follows the gorge rim to an overlook.

- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — upland canopy
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy edges near parking

## Seasonal strategy

**April–June** (★★★): Breeding season. Gorge species singing. Waterfall at good flow. **Note**: Gorge can be closed during high water — check ahead.

**Summer** (★): Swimming destination, very crowded. Not for birding.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 8am–sunset
- **Entry**: Permit required for gorge access (free, apply online)
- **Parking**: Limited — fills early in summer
- **Warning**: Gorge trail requires river crossing and can be closed after rain
- **eBird hotspot**: Cummins Falls State Park

**Best strategy**: Dawn before crowds. Walk rim trail + gorge trail if open. 2 hours. Combine with Burgess Falls (30 min south).
