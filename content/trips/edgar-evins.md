# Edgar Evins State Park

## Why Edgar Evins?

**Center Hill Lake** — a 18,220-acre Corps of Engineers reservoir on the Caney Fork River — with the park occupying a forested peninsula. Edgar Evins is the **largest park in the Eastern Highland Rim batch** at 6,300 acres, with steep forested ridges dropping to the lake.

## The landscape

- **Center Hill Lake** — deep, clear reservoir with long coves
- **Forested peninsula** — the park juts into the lake, giving water access from multiple sides
- **Steep hardwood forest** — oak-hickory-tulip poplar on ridges
- **Lake coves** — sheltered areas for waterfowl

## Birding

### Highland Rim Trail (3 mi — moderate)
Ridge-top trail through upland forest.

- **Wood Thrush** — flute-song in cove forest
- **Northern Parula** — canopy along trail
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — WHEEP from canopy
- **Scarlet Tanager** — high canopy, breeding season

### Marina + Lake Access
Scope the lake from marina area and boat ramp.

- **Osprey** — fishing over the lake (April–September)
- **Bald Eagle** — winter, perched along shoreline
- **Winter waterfowl**: common divers and dabblers
- **Great Blue Heron** — year-round along shore

## Seasonal strategy

**April–May** (★★★★): Breeding season. Forest birds singing. Osprey active on lake.

**November–February** (★★★): Winter waterfowl and eagles on lake.

**Summer** (★★): Recreation lake. Dawn forest birding only.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 60 sites + cabins
- **Marina**: Full-service, boat rentals available
- **eBird hotspot**: Edgar Evins State Park

**Best strategy**: Morning forest trail + lake scope. 3 hours. Combine with Burgess Falls (15 min east).

## Pairing

- **Burgess Falls** (15 min east) — gorge waterfalls
- **Standing Stone** (45 min north) — plateau forest
