# Savage Gulf State Natural Area

## Why Savage Gulf?

The **deepest gorges on the Cumberland Plateau** — 800+ feet deep. Savage Gulf is the wild, trail-only heart of the South Cumberland system. Three gorges converge: Savage Creek, Big Creek, and Collins Gulf. The depth creates **temperature inversions** that support Appalachian species far west of the mountains.

15,500 acres of near-wilderness. State Natural Area designation means **minimal development**.

## Birding

### Savage Day Loop (4.2 mi — moderate)
Follows the gorge rim with overlooks.

- **Cerulean Warbler** — canopy along rim trail. Best Cerulean habitat on the southern plateau
- **Worm-eating Warbler** — steep gorge slopes
- **Hooded Warbler** — understory patches

### Collins Gulf Trail (8 mi round-trip — strenuous)
Descends into the deepest gorge.

- **Black-throated Blue Warbler** — rhododendron thickets at gorge bottom
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — gorge streams
- **Black-throated Green Warbler** — hemlock stands

### Stone Door Trail (2 mi — easy)
Shared trailhead with South Cumberland unit.

- **Cerulean Warbler** — rim-top forest
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy

## Seasonal strategy

**May–June** (★★★★★): Peak breeding. Gorge at its best. All species singing.

**April** (★★★★): Early breeders. Wildflowers in gorge.

**October** (★★★): Fall foliage. Dramatic from rim overlooks.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: Backcountry only (Sawyer, Dinky Line campsites)
- **Trails**: 55+ miles
- **Warning**: Remote backcountry. Carry water, inform someone of your plans.
- **eBird hotspot**: Savage Gulf SNA

**Best strategy**: Savage Day Loop at dawn. 3-4 hours. For gorge-bottom species, descend Collins Gulf (full day).

## Pairing

- **South Cumberland** (adjacent) — Grundy Forest for Swainson's Warbler
- **Fall Creek Falls** (30 min north) — more accessible plateau park
