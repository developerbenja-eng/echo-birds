# Warriors' Path State Park

## Why Warriors' Path?

Named for the Great Cherokee War and Trading Path that once ran through this ridge. The park sits on **Boone Lake** (a TVA reservoir) in the Kingsport area, with ridge forest and lake shoreline. Solid general birding for the far northeastern corner of Tennessee.

## Birding

- **Osprey** — fishing over Boone Lake
- **Bald Eagle** — winter resident
- **Northern Parula** — forest canopy (breeding)
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — WHEEP from canopy
- **Winter waterfowl** on Boone Lake

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 135 sites
- **Trails**: 9 miles
- **Marina**: Boat ramp for lake access
- **eBird hotspot**: Warriors' Path SP

**Best strategy**: Morning forest trail + lake scope. 2-3 hours.

## Pairing

- **Sycamore Shoals** (30 min east) — Watauga River
- **David Crockett Birthplace** (1 hr south) — Nolichucky River
