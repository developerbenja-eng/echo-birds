# Fort Pillow State Historic Park — Trip Guide

> 1,642 acres of **Chickasaw Bluffs + bottomland + historic Civil War fort** on the Mississippi River north of Memphis (~1 hour drive). Primary value is **history**, but the bluff-top setting makes it a decent **migration watchpoint**.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Fort Pillow is a **historic site first, birding location second**. The park is organized around the 1864 Battle of Fort Pillow site (and the civil-war earthworks on the bluff). But the **bluff-top position + mature forest + river-adjacent bottomland** creates productive habitat:

- **Migration corridor view** along the Chickasaw Bluff
- **Big mature trees** around the historic site
- **Bottomland forest** below the bluff
- **Mississippi River** viewing from overlooks

---

## Target species

Largely the same as Meeman-Shelby Forest:
- **Bluff-top migrants** (fall hawk watch potential — Broad-winged Hawks, Red-tailed)
- **Pileated Woodpecker**, **Barred Owl**, **Summer Tanager** in mature bluff forest
- **Prothonotary Warbler** in the bottomland
- **Mississippi Kite** (summer, riverbank)
- **Chimney Swift** (dusk)
- Fall warbler migration along the bluffs

---

## Where to bird

- **Bluff-top trails** near the reconstructed fort — mature hardwood, migration watching
- **Overlook trail** — view the river + bottomland
- **Lower trail** (if open) — bottomland forest birds

---

## When to visit

**Fall migration (Sep–Oct)** is the park's best birding window. **Migrant passerines pile up** on the bluff edge. Hawk migration uses the Mississippi River corridor — Broad-winged kettles pass overhead in mid-September.

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~1 hr north via US-51 → TN-87
- **County**: Lauderdale
- **Combine with**: Meeman-Shelby Forest (you pass near it) for a full bluffs day
- **Historical interest**: Civil War battlefield + interpretive center
