# Ocoee River State Park

## Why Ocoee River?

**Tennessee's newest state park** (designated 2025) — built around the **1996 Olympic whitewater venue** on the Ocoee River in the Cherokee National Forest. The Ocoee hosted the Olympic and Paralympic kayak/canoe events during the Atlanta Games.

Primarily a **recreation park** (whitewater rafting, kayaking), with forest birding as a secondary activity along the river corridor.

## Birding

- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky stream edges along the river
- **Northern Parula** — forest canopy
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy
- **Black-throated Green Warbler** — hemlock pockets along river gorge
- **Belted Kingfisher** — river corridor

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Primary activity**: Whitewater rafting (commercial outfitters)
- **Trails**: Forest trails along river corridor
- **eBird hotspot**: Ocoee River area

**Best strategy**: Dawn forest walk before rafting crowds arrive. 1-2 hours. Combine with Hiwassee (20 min north).
