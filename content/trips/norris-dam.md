# Norris Dam State Park

## Why Norris Dam?

The **first TVA dam** (1936) — a Depression-era engineering landmark on the Clinch River. The park's 4,000 acres include Norris Lake shoreline, Clinch River bottomland, and forested ridges. The combination of **lake + river + ridge forest** supports both **winter waterfowl** and **breeding warblers** (including Cerulean Warbler on the ridge-top forest).

## Birding

### Clinch River Greenway
- **Bald Eagle** — winter residents along the river
- **Osprey** — fishing over Norris Lake (spring/summer)
- **Belted Kingfisher** — river resident
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky stream edges

### Ridge trails (5+ mi)
- **Cerulean Warbler** — ridge-top canopy (breeding May–July)
- **Northern Parula** — canopy singer
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — WHEEP from forest

### Lake access
- **Winter waterfowl**: Common Loon, diving ducks, grebes
- **Great Blue Heron** — year-round

## Seasonal strategy

**November–February** (★★★★): Winter eagles + waterfowl on lake/river.

**May–June** (★★★★): Cerulean Warbler on ridges. Breeding season.

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 75 sites + cabins
- **Trails**: 15+ miles
- **eBird hotspot**: Norris Dam State Park

**Best strategy**: Winter — river walk for eagles. Spring — ridge trails for Cerulean.

## Pairing

- **Big Ridge** (20 min east) — Norris Lake CCC park
- **Frozen Head** (1 hr southwest) — Cumberland Plateau
