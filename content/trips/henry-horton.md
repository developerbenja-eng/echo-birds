# Henry Horton State Park

## Why Henry Horton?

**The Duck River** — one of the most biodiverse rivers in North America — flows through the heart of this park. The Duck holds **more fish species per mile** than any river in North America and supports a correspondingly rich riparian bird community. Henry Horton gives you easy access to this exceptional waterway.

1,523 acres of **Duck River bottomland, upland forest, and open fields** in the rolling hills of Marshall County.

## The landscape

- **Duck River corridor** — rocky shoals, deep pools, forested banks. The signature habitat
- **Bottomland hardwood** — sycamore, cottonwood, river birch lining the banks
- **Upland oak-hickory forest** — rolling terrain above the river
- **Open fields + edges** — old agricultural land reverting to forest
- **Golf course + resort area** — landscaped grounds that attract open-country species

## Birding routes

### Duck River Trail (2 mi — easy)
Follows the river through bottomland forest. The core birding route.

- **Belted Kingfisher** — resident along the river, rattling call year-round
- **Warbling Vireo** — singing from sycamores May–August
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — walks along rocky shoals, arrives late March
- **Wood Duck** — in quiet backwater pools
- **Great Blue Heron** — wading in shallows

### Upland Forest Loop (1.5 mi — moderate)
Climbs into oak-hickory forest above the river.

- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy WHEEP calls
- **Northern Parula** — rising buzz from treetops
- **Eastern Towhee** — scratching in understory leaf litter, "drink-your-tea"
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — loud calls echo through forest

### Park roads + fields
Drive or walk the park roads at dawn.

- **Eastern Bluebird** — fence posts and open areas
- **Eastern Meadowlark** — fields (declining, treasure each sighting)
- **Red-tailed Hawk** — soaring over fields

## Seasonal strategy

**April–June** (★★★★): Breeding season peak. All river species active. Warbling Vireo + waterthrush singing. Wildflowers along river.

**November–January** (★★★): Winter — scan river for lingering waterfowl. Kingfisher resident. Winter sparrows in brushy edges.

**July–September** (★★): Hot + quiet. Dawn only.
