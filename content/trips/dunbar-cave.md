# Dunbar Cave State Park

## Why Dunbar Cave?

**A prehistoric cave with 2,000-year-old Native American art** surrounded by a small but productive forest park on the edge of Clarksville. Dunbar Cave is one of the largest caves in the region — **Chimney Swifts historically roosted in the cave entrance** in huge numbers, and the cave + surrounding forest create a compact birding site.

At only 144 acres, this is a quick stop rather than a full-day destination — but the cave is remarkable and the forest is good.

## The landscape

- **Dunbar Cave entrance** — massive cave mouth, 15 ft high × 100 ft wide
- **Swan Lake** — small spring-fed lake at cave base
- **Mature deciduous forest** — hardwoods on limestone karst
- **Cave entrance zone** — twilight habitat with unique microclimate

## Birding

### Cave Trail + Lake Loop (1 mi — easy)
Walk from parking to cave entrance past Swan Lake.

- **Chimney Swift** — swirling overhead; historically roosted in cave (now shifted to urban chimneys)
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — forest canopy
- **Northern Parula** — lakeside trees
- **Eastern Phoebe** — cave entrance area (phoebes love cave overhangs)

### Forest Loop (1.5 mi — moderate)
Trail through upland forest above the cave.

- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy understory
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — dead trees in mature forest

## Seasonal strategy

**April–May** (★★★): Spring migration + breeding. Swift activity. Forest birds singing.

**September–October** (★★★): Fall migration. Swift staging.

**Summer/Winter** (★★): Limited birding value — worth it for the cave, not specifically the birds.

## The cave

The cave tour (seasonal, fee) reveals **2,000-year-old Mississippian and Woodland-era Native American petroglyphs** — some of the best-preserved cave art in the Southeast. The cave extends 8+ miles underground. Even without the tour, the massive entrance is impressive.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 8am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free (cave tour has fee)
- **Cave tours**: Seasonal (April–October), check schedule
- **Trails**: ~3 miles
- **eBird hotspot**: Dunbar Cave State Park

**Best strategy**: 1-2 hour stop. Walk to cave, loop the forest trail. Combine with a cave tour if available.

## Pairing

- **Port Royal** (20 min northeast) — Red River riparian
- **Harpeth River** (40 min south) — river corridor birding
