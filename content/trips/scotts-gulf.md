# Scott's Gulf Wilderness

## Why Scott's Gulf?

A **remote, undeveloped canyon system** on the Caney Fork River — 10,000 acres of near-wilderness with cliffs, waterfalls, and old-growth forest. This is **Tennessee's wildest state natural area** — no facilities, primitive trails, and serious backcountry. For experienced hikers who bird.

## Birding

The canyon supports the same plateau gorge community as Savage Gulf:

- **Cerulean Warbler** — gorge-rim canopy
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — Caney Fork stream edges
- **Worm-eating Warbler** — steep canyon slopes
- **Hooded Warbler** — dense understory

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk
- **Entry**: Free
- **Facilities**: None — primitive backcountry only
- **Warning**: Very remote. No marked trails in some areas. Navigation skills required. Not for beginners.
- **eBird hotspot**: Scott's Gulf SNA

**Best strategy**: Only for experienced backcountry hikers. Day hike from rim trailheads. Combine with Rock Island (nearby).
