# Rock Island State Park

## Why Rock Island?

Where the **Caney Fork River, Collins River, and Rocky River** converge — creating a dramatic landscape of **gorges, waterfalls, and deep blue pools**. The Great Falls Dam creates a broad cascade visible from the park. Three rivers meeting = diverse riparian habitat.

883 acres of river gorge, upland forest, and lake shoreline.

## Birding

### Great Falls Trail (1 mi — easy to moderate)
Views of the dam and gorge.

- **Belted Kingfisher** — rattling along the rivers
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky stream edges below falls
- **Great Blue Heron** — wading in pools

### Upstream Flat Trail (2 mi — easy)
Follows the Collins River upstream through forest.

- **Northern Parula** — streamside canopy
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy calling
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest

### Lake access
Center Hill Lake is accessible from the park.

- **Winter waterfowl** on lake coves
- **Bald Eagle** — winter, scanning from tall trees

## Seasonal strategy

**April–May** (★★★): Breeding season. River species singing.

**November–February** (★★★): Winter waterfowl on lake. Eagles.

**Summer** (★★): Swimming/kayaking destination. Dawn birding only.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 50 sites + cabins
- **eBird hotspot**: Rock Island State Park

**Best strategy**: Morning river walk + falls viewpoint. 2 hours. Combine with Burgess Falls (20 min north).
