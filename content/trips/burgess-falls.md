# Burgess Falls State Park

## Why Burgess Falls?

**Four cascading waterfalls** along the Falling Water River, dropping 250 feet through a forested gorge to Center Hill Lake. The gorge microclimate supports **Appalachian-influenced species** farther west than expected — including Black-throated Green Warbler, which breeds in the cool hemlock-and-rhododendron ravines.

Only 350 acres, but the **deep gorge habitat** punches above its weight for birding.

## The landscape

- **Falling Water River gorge** — 250-ft drop over 1.5 miles with four major waterfalls
- **Hemlock-rhododendron ravines** — cool, moist microhabitat supporting highland species
- **Upland hardwood forest** on the rim
- **Center Hill Lake** at the gorge mouth

## Birding routes

### Waterfall Trail (1.5 mi one-way — moderate, some stairs)
The signature hike, following the gorge rim past all four falls.

- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — gorge stream edges, arrives late March. The cascading creek is perfect waterthrush habitat
- **Black-throated Green Warbler** — hemlock ravines along the gorge. Listen for "zee-zee-zee-zoo-zee"
- **Northern Parula** — canopy above gorge trail
- **Wood Thrush** — cove forest sections, flute-song echoes in gorge
- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — upland canopy

### River Trail (short spur)
Access to the river above the falls.

- **Belted Kingfisher** — rattling along the river
- **Great Blue Heron** — wading in pools above falls

## Seasonal strategy

**April–June** (★★★★): Breeding season peak. Waterthrush + warblers singing. Waterfalls at good flow.

**September–October** (★★★): Fall migration through gorge corridor. Foliage.

**Winter** (★★): Quiet. Waterfalls dramatic after rain. Kingfisher + woodpeckers resident.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 8am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Trails**: ~2.5 miles
- **Parking**: Can fill on weekends (popular swimming hole)
- **eBird hotspot**: Burgess Falls State Park

**Best strategy**: Dawn walk on Waterfall Trail before crowds arrive. 2 hours. Listen for waterthrush along stream.

## Pairing

- **Cummins Falls** (30 min north) — another gorge waterfall
- **Rock Island** (20 min south) — river confluence
- **Edgar Evins** (15 min west) — Center Hill Lake
