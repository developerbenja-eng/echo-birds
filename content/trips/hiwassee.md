# Hiwassee Scenic River State Park

## Why Hiwassee?

The **Hiwassee River** is one of Tennessee's clearest and most beautiful mountain rivers — and the adjacent **Hiwassee Wildlife Refuge** hosts the largest **Sandhill Crane** wintering concentration in Tennessee. Thousands of cranes gather from November through February.

## Birding

### Hiwassee River
- **Belted Kingfisher** — rattling along the river
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — rocky edges (breeding season)
- **Great Blue Heron** — wading

### Hiwassee Wildlife Refuge (adjacent, TWRA)
- **Sandhill Crane** — **thousands** winter here (November–February). Best viewing from observation platform
- **Waterfowl** — the refuge marshes support wintering ducks
- **Bald Eagle** — winter resident

## The crane story

The **Cherokee National Forest Sandhill Crane wintering area** near the Hiwassee River is one of the most important crane sites east of the Mississippi. Cranes stage here during migration, and a growing population winters. The **Tennessee Sandhill Crane Festival** (January) celebrates the spectacle.

## Logistics

- **Entry**: Free
- **Trails**: Short (park is small)
- **Crane viewing**: Best from TWRA refuge observation area (adjacent to park)
- **Festival**: January — Tennessee Sandhill Crane Festival
- **eBird hotspot**: Hiwassee Wildlife Refuge + Hiwassee Scenic River SP

**Best strategy**: Winter — crane viewing at refuge. Spring — river birding. 2-3 hours.

## Pairing

- **Ocoee River** (20 min south) — whitewater corridor
- **Red Clay** (1 hr north) — Cherokee historic site
