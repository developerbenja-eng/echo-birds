# North Chickamauga Creek Gorge

## Why North Chickamauga?

**7,000 acres of plateau gorge** just north of Chattanooga — accessible Cerulean Warbler habitat within reach of Tennessee's fourth-largest city. The gorge cuts through the plateau edge, creating the classic hemlock-lined canyon environment.

## Birding

- **Cerulean Warbler** — gorge-rim canopy, breeding
- **Worm-eating Warbler** — steep gorge slopes with leaf litter
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** — creek edges in gorge
- **Hooded Warbler** — dense understory
- **Black-throated Green Warbler** — hemlock pockets

## Logistics

- **Hours**: Dawn to dusk
- **Entry**: Free
- **Trails**: 6+ miles
- **Access**: Multiple trailheads off Dayton Pike
- **eBird hotspot**: North Chickamauga Creek Gorge SNA

**Best strategy**: Morning gorge-rim walk. 3 hours. Combine with Chattanooga-area birding (Raccoon Mountain, Tennessee Aquarium area).
