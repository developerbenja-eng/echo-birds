# Tims Ford State Park

## Why Tims Ford?

**Tims Ford Lake** — a 10,700-acre reservoir on the Elk River — is one of the best **winter waterfowl and eagle** lakes in Middle Tennessee. The park's 3,546 acres include lakefront forest, open shoreline, and upland hardwoods.

## The landscape

- **Tims Ford Lake** — deep, clear reservoir with rocky shoreline
- **Lake coves** — sheltered inlets where waterfowl concentrate
- **Upland hardwood forest** — oak-hickory on ridges above lake
- **Open areas** — marina, golf course, picnic grounds

## Birding

### Shoreline access / Marina
Scope the lake from multiple shoreline access points.

- **Bald Eagle** — winter residents, perched in tall lakeside trees
- **Common Loon** — winter visitor on deep water (December–March)
- **Osprey** — spring/summer, fishing over the lake
- **Winter waterfowl**: Bufflehead, Ring-necked Duck, Lesser Scaup, Hooded Merganser

### Forest trails (3+ mi)
Upland forest trails above the lake.

- **Great Crested Flycatcher** — canopy, breeding season
- **Northern Parula** — lakeside forest canopy
- **Eastern Towhee** — brushy edges
- **Pileated Woodpecker** — mature forest

## Seasonal strategy

**November–March** (★★★★): Winter peak. Eagle + loon + waterfowl on lake.

**April–May** (★★★): Osprey returns. Breeding forest birds.

**Summer** (★★): Recreation focus. Dawn birding only.

## Logistics

- **Hours**: 7am–sunset
- **Entry**: Free
- **Camping**: 50 sites + cabins
- **Marina**: Boat ramp for lake access
- **eBird hotspot**: Tims Ford State Park

**Best strategy**: Winter morning — scope lake from marina + shoreline for eagles and loons. Combine with southern Middle TN parks.

## Pairing
