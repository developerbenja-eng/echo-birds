# Reelfoot Lake State Park — Trip Guide

> **The Bald Eagle capital of Tennessee.** 15,000 acres of cypress-tupelo swamp + shallow natural lake in the extreme northwest corner of the state — 2 hours north of Memphis. Hosts 300+ bird species and **400+ wintering Bald Eagles**.

---

## Why it's different from Memphis

Memphis sits on the **Mississippi alluvial plain** — but Memphis is a city. Reelfoot is the **alluvial plain in its natural state**, just 90 miles upriver. The habitat is similar to Memphis's Wolf River and Meeman-Shelby bottoms, but at a **completely different scale**:

- **15,000 acres of continuous swamp + lake** (vs. Memphis's fragmented bottomland parks)
- **Standing dead cypress "knees"** across 18,000 acres of shallow water
- **Major Mississippi Flyway stopover** — much bigger than any Memphis bottom
- **Adjacent to the Mississippi River** — birds funnel through here during migration

The most unique feature: **Reelfoot is a 1811–1812 earthquake lake.** The **New Madrid earthquakes** (the largest in recorded U.S. history) dropped the ground here, and the Mississippi River **briefly ran backward** to fill the basin. The lake's drowned cypress forest dates to that event — those dead trees are **200+ years old** and still standing.

---

## The eagle spectacle

**November through March**, Reelfoot hosts **the largest wintering Bald Eagle concentration in the interior South** — typically **200–400 birds**, sometimes more. The **Tennessee State Parks Eagle Festival** in late January draws thousands of visitors for guided pontoon eagle tours.

- **Peak**: **mid-January**
- **Where**: north and east sides of the lake (Blue Basin, Walnut Log area)
- **How**: ranger-led bus + pontoon tours available through park office in Jan-Feb
- **Bonus**: you'll also see **bald eagles fighting, courting, mating** — nesting starts in February

No other TN site comes close for Bald Eagle density in winter.

## Target species you won't find in Memphis

### Winter (Dec–Feb) — the blockbuster season
- **Bald Eagle** (200–400 birds — far more than Memphis riverfront)
- **American White Pelican** (thousands in late-fall migration; winter flocks too)
- **Sandhill Crane** (reliable winter flocks near the lake)
- **Snow Goose** (big flocks in peak winter)
- **Ross's Goose** (mixed with Snow Geese)
- **Canvasback**, **Redhead**, **Common Goldeneye**, **Hooded Merganser** — diving ducks in numbers
- **Tundra Swan** (small flocks possible)

### Summer breeders (May–Jul) — the swamp specialists
- **Anhinga** (at the edge of its range; rare elsewhere in TN)
- **Least Bittern** (small dark heron; cattail marshes)
- **Purple Gallinule** (vivid purple rail; rare wanderer)
- **Common Gallinule** (more regular; lily-pad marshes)
- **Swainson's Warbler** (dense cane + bottomland understory)
- **Black-crowned & Yellow-crowned Night-Heron** (both species present)
- **Prothonotary Warbler** (abundant — the swamp is golden with them)
- **Mississippi Kite** (overhead)

### Migration — the flyway funnel
- Massive waterfowl pushes in **October–December** and **February–March**
- Warbler migration peaks late April
- Shorebird migration at lake-edge mudflats (late summer drawdowns)

## Where to bird at Reelfoot

### Blue Basin (northwest lake)
Best eagle viewing in winter. Open water with surrounding cypress stumps. **Large eagle roosts** in cypress snags. Easy road access along TN-213.

### Walnut Log (northeast)
Classic cypress swamp viewing. Best for **Prothonotary Warblers in May-June**, plus herons, wood ducks, and kingfishers.

### Black Bayou / Kirby Pocket
Deeper swamp with **Anhinga + Least Bittern** in summer. Slower exploration by canoe or kayak.

### Reelfoot Lake State Park Visitor Center
Boardwalks + auditorium birding presentations. Ranger-led tours leave from here.

### Air Park Inn area
Shore birding + accessible shallow water.

## When to visit

| Season | What to look for |
|---|---|
| **Jan–Feb (PEAK)** | Bald Eagles (400+), winter waterfowl, eagle festival, sandhill cranes |
| **Mar** | Waterfowl pushing north, early-arriving Prothonotary Warblers |
| **Apr–May** | Warbler migration, breeding swamp species setting up territories |
| **Jun–Jul** | Anhinga, Least Bittern, Prothonotary at peak, Swainson's Warbler |
| **Aug–Sep** | Shorebirds at drawn-down mudflats, post-breeding herons |
| **Oct–Nov** | Waterfowl arriving, first eagles returning, American White Pelican flocks |
| **Dec** | Full winter wildlife assembling, eagles staking out territories |

## How to bird Reelfoot

**For eagles (winter)**: drive the perimeter roads with binoculars. TN-213 on the north side has the most eagle-in-cypress photo opportunities. Arrive by 8am for morning flight activity.

**For swamp birds (summer)**: rent a canoe or johnboat at the park (or bring your own). The **interior cypress cells** are where Anhinga, Least Bittern, and the rare stuff hide. Or walk the boardwalks at dawn.

**For migration**: check eBird before going — mudflats + passage depend on water levels.

**Eagle Festival** (late Jan) is the easiest introduction — reserved pontoon tours with rangers. Book a month ahead.

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~2 hours north via I-155 → US-51
- **Nearest town**: Tiptonville (park HQ), Samburg (restaurants)
- **Lodging**: Reelfoot Lake State Park resort rooms + cabins, or Tiptonville motels
- **Park admission**: Free (statewide TN policy)
- **Ranger tours**: Jan–Feb eagle tours, book through park office
- **Bring**: boots or water shoes, insect repellent (summer), binoculars + scope (critical), waterfowl field guide

## Why these species are HERE and not Memphis

| Species | Why Reelfoot > Memphis |
|---|---|
| Bald Eagle (winter) | **Vast shallow fish lake** — eagles need open water + dead perches; Memphis has neither at scale |
| Anhinga | **Right at northern range edge** — needs warm shallow swamp; Memphis is JUST north of range |
| Least Bittern | **Extensive cattail marshes**; Memphis wetlands too small/drained |
| Purple Gallinule | **Lily-pad marshes** — Memphis lacks this habitat entirely |
| Swainson's Warbler | **Thick cane breaks + bottomland** — Memphis bottoms too thin |
| Snow/Ross's Goose | **Mississippi Flyway bottleneck** — 400k+ geese funnel through Reelfoot |
| Tundra Swan | **Shallow lake wintering habitat**; doesn't use Memphis riverfront |

Memphis has **waterfowl on the Mississippi River**. Reelfoot has **waterfowl on a shallow flooded forest** — totally different experience.
