# Tennessee State Parks — Birding Master List

**62 state parks** covering ~200,000 acres across Tennessee's three grand regions. All 100% free admission (statewide policy since 2006). This is the master index of the birding-focused trip-guide project — each park gets its own dedicated trip guide over time.

**Ecoregions: Mississippi Alluvial Plain · Coastal Plain · Loess Bluffs**

## What makes a park-by-park guide worthwhile

Each park guide should answer:
1. **Why is this park's bird community different** from Memphis / adjacent parks?
2. **Target species** you won't see elsewhere in TN (or see more reliably here)
3. **Best trails** for birding
4. **Seasonal timing** — when to go
5. **Habitat breakdown** — why those birds are there

The goal is to build up a **Tennessee Birding Atlas** over time that helps a Memphis-based birder plan drives of 1-6 hours to add species to their state life list.
