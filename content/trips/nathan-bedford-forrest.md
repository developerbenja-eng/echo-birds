# Nathan Bedford Forrest State Park — Trip Guide

> 2,587 acres on **Kentucky Lake's southern bluffs** in Benton County, ~2 hours northeast of Memphis. One of Tennessee's best **winter Bald Eagle viewing sites** outside Reelfoot.

---

## Why visit as a birder

Nathan Bedford Forrest sits on the **Kentucky Lake (Tennessee River) bluffs** — high bluff views over one of the largest reservoirs in the East. The park's **Pilot Knob** (elevation ~700 ft) is a dramatic overlook that creates a **natural hawk migration corridor** + winter eagle watchpoint.

This gives you:
- **Winter Bald Eagle viewing** from the bluff + overlooks
- **Kentucky Lake waterfowl** (thousands of ducks in winter)
- **Fall hawk migration** off the bluff
- **Mature upland forest** for summer breeders

---

## Target species

### Winter (Nov–Feb) — peak season
- **Bald Eagle** (reliable; 20+ winter roost)
- **Osprey** (winter residents on Kentucky Lake)
- **Hooded Merganser**, **Bufflehead**, **Common Goldeneye**, **Ring-necked Duck**, **Canvasback**, **Redhead**
- **American White Pelican** (late fall into winter)
- **Horned Grebe**, **Pied-billed Grebe**
- **Common Loon** (deep water)
- **Ring-billed Gull**, occasional **Bonaparte's Gull**

### Summer breeders
- **Osprey** (lake + platforms)
- **Wood Thrush**, **Summer Tanager**, **Scarlet Tanager** (forest)
- **Yellow-throated Vireo** (canopy)
- **Louisiana Waterthrush** (bluff streams)
- **Acadian Flycatcher** (ravines)

### Migration
- **Fall hawk migration** from the bluffs (Pilot Knob area)
- Spring warbler migration along the bluffs

---

## Where to bird

### Pilot Knob Overlook
**The marquee birding spot.** High bluff view over Kentucky Lake. Winter eagle scanning, fall hawk watching, scenic vistas.

### Lakeshore Trail
Drops to the water's edge. **Winter waterfowl scoping** with a spotting scope.

### Interior forest trails
Upland oak-hickory for summer breeders.

### Johnsonville Ferry Crossing area (at the north end)
Waterfowl concentrations + Bald Eagle fishing.

---

## When to visit

| Season | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Nov–Feb (PEAK)** | Bald Eagle + waterfowl (bring a spotting scope) |
| **Mar** | Waterfowl pushing north + first Osprey |
| **Apr–May** | Spring warbler migration along bluffs |
| **Jun–Jul** | Breeding Osprey, summer forest species |
| **Sep** | Fall hawk migration off Pilot Knob |

---

## Why it's a Kentucky Lake must-stop

- **Highest bluff overlook** on Kentucky Lake
- **Reliable winter Bald Eagle** at a closer drive than Reelfoot
- **Diverse waterfowl** in winter
- **Pairs well** with Johnsonville State Park (directly across the lake)

---

## Logistics

- **Drive from Memphis**: ~2 hr northeast via I-40 → US-641
- **County**: Benton (near Eva, TN)
- **Amenities**: cabins, inn, marina
- **Admission**: Free
- **Bring**: spotting scope (critical for winter waterfowl)
